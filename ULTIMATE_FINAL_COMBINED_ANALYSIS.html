üéì Complete Guide to Detecting Manipulation

1
Identify Authority Claims
Look for: "must," "shall," "required," "studies show," "experts agree," "research confirms." These create illusion of authority. Always ask: Says who? What law? Which studies? Which experts?
2
Check for Evidence
Real claims have: Author names (Smith et al., 2024), Journal names (Nature, Science), Law citations (42 USC 1983), Page numbers, DOIs, URLs. No citation = likely false.
3
Question Vague Quantifiers
"Significant," "substantial," "numerous," "many" hide real numbers. Always demand: How many exactly? What percentage? Compared to what?
4
Verify Legal Language
"Shall," "must," "required" need legal backing. Ask: What specific statute? What regulation? What happens if not followed? No law cited = manipulation.
5
Spot False Urgency
"Critical," "vital," "immediate," "urgent" create panic. Ask: What's the timeline? What happens if delayed? Where's the emergency? Usually there is none.
